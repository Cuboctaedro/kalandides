Time in transit becomes time in contact with the surroundings, not only a gap to be endured. The body spends more time sitting, and time in transit is often spent in traffic or in isolated compartments. The city is experienced through windshields, brief entries and exits, and parking lots. In other areas, basic services are farther away, pavements are broken or absent, and traffic is more dangerous. The capacity to use walking as a mode of everyday inhabitation is therefore unevenly distributed. Recognising this difference does not provide an immediate solution, but it clarifies that urban form is not neutral.
