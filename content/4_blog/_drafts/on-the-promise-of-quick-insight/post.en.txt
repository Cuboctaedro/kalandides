It may alter how a situation is interpreted, without significantly affecting how it is enacted over time.</p>"},"id":"58eddf80-cb71-4e30-a0a2-4883b311ca1a","isHidden":false,"type":"text"},{"content":{"text":"<p>This raises questions about the role that insight is expected to play within coaching. Breakthroughs are easier to communicate than slow transformations. They fit well within formats that privilege clarity, resolution, and identifiable turning points. This can shape expectations on both sides of the coaching relationship, creating pressure for outcomes that are difficult to produce in a consistent or meaningful way.</p>"},"id":"18a0a694-873d-49fc-80a5-4c586957fc98","isHidden":false,"type":"text"},{"content":{"text":"<p>From a systemic perspective, this orientation appears limited.
