The second is <strong>humanistic geography</strong>, with its attention to place, attachment, insideness, outsideness, and placelessness. The third concerns <strong>relational and critical approaches to space</strong>, which emphasise that environments are produced through social relations, power, and differential access. The fourth focuses on the entanglement of <strong>time, space, and embodiment</strong>, and on how lived temporality shapes spatial experience and vice versa.
